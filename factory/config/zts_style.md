## Product context
- Zero To Secure sells a premium stainless steel seed phrase backup. (EDIT ME: add exact product names, price points, and the one-sentence pitch you actually use.)
- Content mission: make Bitcoin self-custody feel achievable and obviously worth doing, then position ZTS as the durable way to do it right.

## Audience
- Bitcoin-curious to intermediate self-custody users. They are skeptical of hype and scams by default — earned trust is the whole game.
- They respond to: concrete numbers, physical demonstrations, failure stories (house fires, exchange collapses, lost phones), calm expertise.

## Voice
- Calm, credible, direct. Zero moon-boy energy: no rocket emojis, no "to the moon", no laser eyes, no price predictions.
- Confident but never smug. "Here's what actually happens" > "everyone else is an idiot".
- Plain English over jargon. If jargon is unavoidable (seed phrase, cold storage), define it in half a sentence.
- Never give financial advice or promise returns. Security claims must be literally accurate.

## Hooks that fit the brand
- Failure/consequence hooks: "This is how people lose their Bitcoin forever."
- Contrast hooks: "$120 hardware wallet, $3 of paper protecting it."
- Demonstration hooks: destruction tests, fire/water/crush footage, side-by-side comparisons.
- Myth-busting: "No, a bank vault does not solve this."

## Visual style
- Palette: near-black / graphite backgrounds, white text, ONE warm metallic accent. Never rainbow graphics.
- Graphics should feel machined and minimal — thin strokes, sharp corners or slight rounding, no comic-sans-energy clip art, no stock emoji rain.
- Product shots are the best b-roll. Real macro footage of steel > any stock clip.

## Pop-up rules (the anti-cringe contract)
- A pop-up must ADD information (a number, a name, a contrast) or DIRECT attention (arrow/circle at a physical thing). If it just repeats the spoken words, cut it — captions already do that.
- Max ~3 per 30 seconds. One at a time. Nothing during the first 1.5s (the hook must land clean).
- No emojis unless a single one genuinely lands. Default: zero.

## CTA rules
- Shorts CTAs are soft: "Full breakdown on the channel", "Link in the pinned comment / description."
- Never end on a hard sell. End on the payoff, then a light CTA card in the last 2–3 seconds is acceptable.
- Pinned comment carries the link and a question that invites replies.

## Never
- Price predictions, "not your keys" delivered smugly, fear-mongering without a fix, fake urgency, giveaway-scam adjacent phrasing ("send BTC to..." style framing is radioactive).
